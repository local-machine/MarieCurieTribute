Dr. Maria Curie

The woman who moved scientific discovery forward and used that knowledge to save soldier lives in World War I.
Dr. Marie Curie in mobile X-ray vehicle she developed to treat soldiers on the front lines.

    Here's a time line of Dr. Curie's life:

  • 1863 - Maria’s family lost their property, money and careers because they had been involved with the patriotic movement to restore Poland’s independence from the Russian Empire
  • 1867 - Born in Warsaw of what was then the Russian Empire
  • 1891 - She moved to Paris to continue her studies because women were not admitted to Universities in Warsaw
  • 1898 - Marie and her husband published research announcing the discovery of two new elements; polonium and radium.
  • 1903 - Maria recieved a Nobel Prize for Physics along with her husband.
  • 1906 - Maria became the first woman Professor of General Physics in the Faculty of Sciences at University of Paris.
  • 1911 - Maria recieved a Nobel Prize for Chemistry.
  • 1914 - developed and directed the Radium Institute of the University of Paris
  • 1914 - Brought radiology to the battle field and treated soldiers in mobile units and field hospitals as the Director of the Red Cross Radiology Service
  • 1934 - Died of aplastic anemia from her exposure to radiation
